The Virtual Reality Headset: Relic of a Revolutionary Technology

Rediscovered after several decades, the virtual reality headset takes us back to a time when digital immersion promised to transform our lives. What remains of that vision in the era of brain chips ?

Published on October 5, 2060 | By Vestiges VR |

Category: Technology | 5 comments

The VR Headset: A Total Immersion into the Past

Originally designed to immerse users in digital worlds, the VR headset symbolized a technological revolution. It stood at the crossroads of video games, interactive cinema, and online social experiences, pushing the boundaries of human perception by providing access to fully artificial universes.

"Back then, the promise of the VR headset was huge: traveling through infinite worlds without leaving your home."– Archives, 2030

Today, this technological relic is being rediscovered with curiosity. Once central to education, entertainment, and even medicine, the virtual reality headset had a variety of applications. By examining this object, we aim to understand its impact on the collective imagination and the innovations of that time.

Photo of a woman using a VR headset

From Technological Utopia to Collective Memory

The appeal of virtual reality extended beyond video games. Users attended virtual concerts, explored digitally recreated cities, and interacted remotely through avatars. However, with the rise of implanted brain chips, this first headset—though revolutionary for its time—gradually disappeared from households.

Today, it represents a forgotten technological utopia, rediscovered with fascination. How did this technology shape today’s innovations in immersive experiences and augmented reality ?

“A gateway to infinite worlds”– Advertisement slogan, 2025

Timeline of the VR headset’s evolution:

1957 – The Sensorama, the first concept of a VR headset

1968 – First VR headset, “The Sword of Damocles”

1985 – VPL Research and the "EyePhone"

2012 – Oculus Rift (Kickstarter)

2016 – HTC Vive and PlayStation VR

2030 – Ultra-light VR headset with advanced haptics

2039 – Neuronal VR implants

2050 – Integrated brain chip (Neuro-VR)
